LiFi

What is LiFi?

LiFi is a high speed wireless communication technology that uses visible light to transmit information. It has many similarities to existing WiFi technology, as well as some huge differences. WiFi and LiFi are similar because both technologies are wireless, but also very different, because unlike WiFi, which relies on radio waves, LiFi uses visible light communication or infrared and near-UV spectrum waves. LiFi works by using visible light, like the light that is emitted by any regular lamp or bulb.

Pros of LiFi

  1. Higher speeds than Wi-Fi.
  2. 10000 times the frequency spectrum of radio.
  3. More secure because data cannot be intercepted without a clear line of sight.
  4. Eliminates neighboring network interference.
  5. Does not create interference in sensitive electronics, making it better for use in environments like hospitals and aircraft.

LiFi will play a vital role in the future as technology advances and the technology could enable greater area of coverage than a single WiFi router. Drawbacks to the technology include the need for a clear line of sight, difficulties with mobility and the requirement that lights stay on for operation.
